# Example: L12 Prism on Poetry (Dylan Thomas)

**Command:** `python prism.py --scan "Do not go gentle into that good night..." --use-prism l12_universal`

The l12_universal prism (73 words, domain-neutral) works on any input — code, business, philosophy, poetry.

## Input

Dylan Thomas, "Do not go gentle into that good night" (full villanelle, 19 lines)

## What the prism found

### The Structural Contradiction

> The villanelle form creates a structural contradiction with its own argument. The poem demands rage against death's necessity — but the form *is* death's necessity made beautiful. The refrains must return. The pattern must complete. The poem "goes gentle" structurally while raging semantically.

**The content rages; the structure submits.**

### Three Experts

- **Defender:** The circularity IS the rage — each return to refrain renews assault. The form incarnates persistence.
- **Attacker:** The form domesticates terror. By making death predictable, the poem soothes rather than rages. It's a lullaby about refusing lullabies.
- **Prober:** Both assume form-content is the key. But examine the ADDRESS. The poem pretends intimacy with the father but publishes to the world. Public intimacy, published grief made aesthetic object.

### The Concealment Mechanism

The villanelle conceals its own futility. The form makes rage beautiful — and beauty IS consolation, exactly what the poem claims to refuse.

### The Conservation Law

> Grief must be displaced to be communicated, but not so far it becomes impersonal.

### The Meta-Law

> All structural analysis of art conceals the analyst's need to master what the art reveals about mortality.

The framework catches itself: the analysis performs the same displacement it diagnoses. Analyzing the poem's grief is itself a way of "going gentle" — making death orderly, structural, comprehensible.
