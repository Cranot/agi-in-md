## Conservation Law Application

**What we sacrificed:** Perfect prompt fairness
**What we gained:** System-level fairness

By giving both models identical prompts, we're testing *task fairness*, not *system fairness*. The Prism system is inherently more powerful because it has specialized sub-agents. This would be UNFAIR if we claimed "Haiku alone beats Opus."

But we're making a different claim: "Haiku *augmented by Prism* leverages architectural advantages that Opus cannot match."

The Conservation Law (`Equity × Impact = Constant`) predicts this: you can't have both perfect prompt-level fairness AND dramatic architectural differentiation. We chose architectural honesty over prompt-level fairness.
