## Phase 3: Applying the Diagnostic Framework to Itself

### What the Conservation Law Conceals

The **Audience Reach × Signal Precision × Cognitive Load = Constant** law assumes:
1. **Homogeneous evaluation criteria** across all technical leaders
2. **Balance as optimal**—that maximizing all three dimensions is the goal
3. **Trade-offs are zero-sum**

These assumptions are false:

**Founders and CTOs optimize different axes**: A CTO hiring a senior engineer might prioritize technical credibility above all else, *welcoming* high cognitive load if it proves exceptional depth. A founder seeking a technical co-founder might prioritize commercial signal, accepting reduced technical precision. The best profiles polarize intentionally rather than balance universally.

**The law masks strategic differentiation**: A profile optimized for CTOs should sacrifice accessibility and commercial signal. A profile optimized for founders should sacrifice some technical depth. Attempting to balance for everyone serves no one optimally.

**The law assumes trade-offs are structural**: Some improvements increase multiple dimensions simultaneously. Clear language that accurately describes technical innovation improves credibility without increasing load. The law ignores the possibility of *better* communication rather than *more* or *less*.

**The law hides the concept of audience filtering**: High cognitive load acts as a filter—only those with sufficient technical context invest the time. This can be a feature, not a bug, when targeting senior technical roles.
