Three parallel temporal paths were run on a temporal simulation analysis of Starlette routing.py:

- **Original Path**: Analysis becomes calcified into team mythology (predictions become dogma)
- **Hostile Path**: Analysis becomes a political weapon (used to justify rewrites)
- **Abandoned Path**: Analysis becomes an irrelevant artifact (predictions meaningless without maintenance)

### The Temporal Invariant

The property that persists across ALL temporal evolutions, regardless of starting conditions:

**THE ANALYSIS ITSELF BECOMES PART OF THE SYSTEM IT ANALYZES**

- Any temporal analysis of code is itself a temporal artifact
- The analysis enters the causal chain of the system it examines
- The observer cannot remain outside the system being observed
- The diagnostic changes the system by its very existence

Conservation law: **Observer Effect x Temporal Authority = Constant**

### Temporal Topology

The paths trace a **temporal cone**: narrow base (present), diverging paths, convergence on the invariant.

**Impossible Points:**
- Where the analysis remains purely objective and external
- Where temporal predictions are completely accurate and unchanging
- Where the system evolves independently of the analysis's existence

### Self-Referential Paradox

The invariant doesn't survive its own application. When applied to itself:
- The invariant about analysis becoming part of systems applies to the invariant itself
- The distinction between "analysis" and "system" dissolves
- The invariant creates its own exception: if true, it becomes part of what it describes

The boundary between temporal analysis and temporal reality is a membrane, not a line.
